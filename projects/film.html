Film — Production at Its Core

A cinematic showcase website for a film production company. Built to feel like the work it represents — immersive, bold, and fast. Every design decision serves one goal: make visitors feel the quality before they've seen a single frame.

The Challenge

Making a Static Site Feel Like Cinema

Film production companies live and die by first impressions. The brief was simple but demanding: the site had to communicate craft, motion, and prestige — without a video background that would tank load time on mobile, and without heavy JavaScript that would hurt SEO.

The solution was to achieve cinematic feel purely through typography, motion timing, layout hierarchy, and deliberate use of negative space. No video, no canvas, no WebGL. Pure CSS and GSAP.

How It Was Built

Craft & Technical Decisions

GSAP ScrollTrigger

Each section has an entrance animation tied to scroll position using GSAP's ScrollTrigger plugin. Elements don't just fade in — they move with weight, respecting the physics of cinematic camera movement (slow start, quick settle).

Typography as Design

The headline typeface is treated as a visual element, not just text. Variable font sizes, extreme tracking, and mix-blend-mode effects on text against imagery create the editorial feel of a film title card.

Performance First

Images are lazy-loaded and served at the correct resolution via srcset. GSAP is loaded from CDN with deferred parsing. First Contentful Paint is under 1.2s — unusual for a visually heavy site.

Hover Micro-Interactions

Every interactive element has a custom hover state designed to match the cinematic mood — film reels that spin, project cards that reveal with a curtain wipe, buttons that glow like projection light. All pure CSS, no JavaScript needed.

Responsive Layout

The site reflows from a dramatic widescreen layout (1440px+) down to a clean mobile experience without losing the cinematic identity. Media queries restructure the grid from horizontal panning to vertical stacking.

Dark Mode by Design

The dark palette is not a preference setting — it is the design. Deep blacks and near-black backgrounds make the accent colours (gold, off-white) hit harder. The visual metaphor is the cinema screen in a dark room.

Design philosophy: Every millisecond of animation was timed to a beat — deliberate, controlled, and purposeful. When the animation feels effortless to watch, it's because it was laboured over during build.
AI-Assisted Development

How AI Helped

For a creative, design-heavy project, AI's role shifts from architecture partner to creative sparring partner.

1

Animation Curve Exploration

I described the feeling I wanted — "like a cinema curtain opening slowly then snapping into place" — and asked Claude to suggest GSAP easing curves and duration values. It gave me three options with explanations. I tested all three and went with the second, tweaking the overshoot slightly. Saved hours of trial and error.

2

CSS Layout Debugging

A complex CSS Grid layout on the project reel section broke at a specific viewport width. I described the symptom to ChatGPT, got three candidate causes, identified the right one (implicit grid track creation), and fixed it in under 5 minutes rather than the 30 it would have taken to debug blind.

3

Copywriting Refinement

The hero section headline went through seven iterations. I gave Claude the brand brief — "cinematic, bold, confident, East African production company" — and it generated ten alternatives. None were used verbatim, but two sparked the phrase that became the final headline.
